2017		 LoRa Winterthur
Setup of 6 gateways for the freely available LoRa radio network TTN. The gateways provide the city area of Winterthur with the radio network and form the interface between the radio network and the Internet. The know-how on how to set up the network has been gathered by Fablab Winti since 2016 through test trials.

2015		 International Study Project 2015
The International Study Project 2015 to Cyprus includes the following goals: 1. The students gain a deep insight into the complex topic of the Cyprus conflict. 2. They are sensitized to political issues and can understand and objectively assess the various views and motivations of the political actors in Cyprus. 3. They not only have the opportunity to go on a study trip but also to actively participate in its form and implementation, thus gaining practical experience in co-organizing a project. These goals are achieved by holding two preparatory workshops in April and July, which serve to generate the essential background knowledge on the origins of the conflict for the 10-day study trip from 28.08-06.09. After returning, the findings will be evaluated in a plenary discussion in October and, with the well-founded knowledge, possible future scenarios for the island will be sketched out.

www.junes.org
April
2014		 Freiluftlichtbildschaukommission (FliK)
The open-air cinema is a traditional open-air cinema that has been held for ten years on the ETH Hönggerberg campus. The screenings will take place in 2014 on May 20 and 21 in the penultimate week of the semester. The cinema is organized by the Student Association of ETH Zurich (VSETH). To provide students with a pleasant end to the semester, admission is free and an attempt is made to show at least one premiere each year. About 1000 people attend the screening each evening.

www.freiluft.ethz.ch
December
2013		 Digital Film Projector for the VSETH Filmstelle
The Filmstelle is a commission of the Association of Students of ETH (VSETH). It was founded in 1924 and has since regularly shown appealing and entertaining films. The Filmstelle sees itself as an important part of the cultural life of ETH Zurich.
Currently, all films are shown analog with a 35mm projector. Since new films have only been distributed digitally since 2010 and the 35mm films are no longer being restored and are slowly decaying, the existence of this oldest repertory cinema in Zurich is at risk. For this reason, the e-na'bel Foundation supports the Filmstelle in purchasing a new digital cinema projector.

www.filmstelle.ch
November
2013		 UAEM Europe Conference 2014 in Basel
UAEM (Universities Allied for Essential Medicine) is an interdisciplinary network of students who are committed to global health and equitable access to essential medicines in developing countries at both international and local levels (universities). The UAEM Europe Conference in Basel serves as a platform for the various project groups from different countries to educate themselves on the topic through lectures by experts (MSF, WHO) and workshops, exchange experiences and creative ideas, and tackle new projects together. Thus, it is both a place of international networking and a kind of student think tank to address global health policy issues innovatively.

www.uaem-europe.org

April
2013		 VSOS - SOSETH virtual machine hosting
The goal of the "Project VSOS" is to provide interested students and student organizations with free virtual servers. This allows them to, for example, run their own web or mail server, or store and publish documents and programs there. Students are thus enabled to practically learn how to handle this technology, such as administering a Linux server or the entire cluster infrastructure, with the support and assistance of the VSOS admin team.
A significant portion of this expansion was funded by e-na'bel.

sos.ethz.ch/ressorts/vsos/

2013		 EESTEC LC Zurich
EESTEC is a non-political, non-profit organization by and for students of electrical engineering, computer science, and mechanical engineering from universities across Europe. EESTEC LC Zurich is conducting a workshop in 2013 with the motto Slot Engines Xtreme. The goal is to build an electric motor for a slot car. At the end of the week, the built vehicles can be tested on a racetrack in a competition.
The e-na'bel Foundation supported the implementation of this workshop by EESTEC.

2012		 Student Sandwich Bar ETZetera
ETZetera is a student sandwich bar in the ETZ building of ETH Zurich, offering high-quality food at student-friendly prices. Despite the low prices, emphasis is placed on fresh and high-quality ingredients, and all sandwiches are handmade and sold by volunteer helpers.

December
2011		 New Composition for the Canada Tour of the Academic Orchestra Zurich
The Academic Orchestra Zurich, which is the joint symphony orchestra of ETH and the University of Zurich, is open to all students and assistants. With its high technical and musical level, the AOZ is today one of the best amateur orchestras in Switzerland. Regularly, orchestral works as well as soloist concerts are worked on. One goal is to introduce young, promising soloists. Participation in academic celebrations is also part of the orchestra's activities. Various concert tours abroad have led to successful performances in Prague, Vienna, Amsterdam, Copenhagen, Milan, Imola, Rome, Osaka, Tokyo, Singapore, and Kuala Lumpur. From July 2-14, 2012, the AOZ will conduct a Canada tour and will play, among other things, a new composition by the young Canadian composer, Eric Champagne, which was specially composed for the AOZ. The e-na'bel Foundation partially financed the composition, as this is, in our view, the longest-lasting investment of the project.

www.aoz.ethz.ch/
November
2011		 PCB Milling Machine for the Student Electronics Lab Bastli
The Bastli is the commission of the AMIV (Academic Association of Mechanical and Electrical Engineering Students at ETH), which provides students with good access to the practical side of their studies. The Bastli operates an electronics lab with modern measuring devices, plenty of lab equipment, and tools. Additionally, students benefit from a component shop and extensive expertise. As a new service, PCBs can be efficiently and cost-effectively manufactured on a PCB milling machine, which was co-financed by the e-na'bel Foundation.

www.bastli.ethz.ch
January
2011		 Network Bar
The Network Bar is an association to promote interpersonal contacts in the student residence Bülachstrasse. The bar follows the non-profit idea, is run voluntarily by students, and offers drinks at moderate prices every Wednesday evening. The goal of the bar is to provide students with an atmosphere to leave behind the student everyday life with a drink and to get to know each other.
Time has left its marks on the bar and the furniture after several years of use, making a renovation necessary. The e-na'bel Foundation partially supported this renovation.
